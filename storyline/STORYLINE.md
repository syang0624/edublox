# Edublox -- Storyline Bible

## The Setting

The year is 2247. Humanity has established a network of research outposts across the solar system. The **Cosmic Academy** trains young cadets to become Stellar Scholars -- explorers who solve scientific mysteries across the universe. Each cadet is assigned to a three-zone sector:

1. **Orbital Station Helios** -- A massive space station orbiting between Mars and Jupiter. This is home base: the command deck, research labs, and mission briefing rooms. NPCs here are senior officers and scientists.

2. **Planet Verdis** -- A recently discovered alien world with bioluminescent landscapes, floating rock formations, and ancient data archives left by a long-gone civilization. Puzzles and research logs are found here.

3. **The Shardfield** -- A dense asteroid belt rich with rare crystals, frozen comet fragments, and drifting satellite wreckage. Cadets explore by hopping between platforms to scan and collect scientific specimens.

## Narrative Arc (Per Session)

Every session follows the same three-act structure, regardless of the study subject:

**Act 1 -- The Briefing (Orbital Station, Dialogue Mission)**
The cadet arrives at Orbital Station Helios and meets an NPC who presents a mystery or problem tied to the study material. The NPC asks questions to test the cadet's understanding. The cadet must answer correctly to unlock the next mission.

**Act 2 -- The Investigation (Planet Verdis, Puzzle Mission)**
The cadet travels to Planet Verdis to investigate. They find scattered research logs or data fragments that must be arranged in the correct order. This tests sequencing, cause-and-effect, or classification knowledge from the source material.

**Act 3 -- The Discovery (The Shardfield, Exploration Mission)**
The cadet ventures into the asteroid field to collect specimens or scan objects that relate to the lesson. Each correct scan reveals a piece of knowledge. Collecting all targets completes the mission and unlocks the session summary.

## Tone Guidelines

- Encouraging, never punishing. Wrong answers get gentle redirects, not failure screens.
- Language is age-appropriate (9-12). No jargon without context.
- The cosmic setting is a wrapper -- the real learning comes from the source material.
- NPCs never break character. They frame academic concepts as space discoveries.
- Humor is light and nerdy. Puns about space are acceptable.
